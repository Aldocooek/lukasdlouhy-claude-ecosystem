# Writing Style System — Direct, Numeric, Human

A methodology for building and encoding a distinctive writing voice. Can be applied to any personal brand or founder communication style.

---

## Core Principles (10 Rules)

1. **Max 2 sentences per idea.** If it needs a third sentence, split the idea or cut it.
2. **Numbers replace adjectives.** Use "40M" instead of "huge success." Use "174 clients" instead of "many clients." Specific numbers are perceived as facts; adjectives are perceived as spin.
3. **Active voice.** "We shipped X" not "X was shipped by us."
4. **Zero hedging language.** Remove: "possibly," "I dare say," "if I may," "perhaps," "maybe." State the thing.
5. **Personalization in the opening line.** Cold outreach opens with something specific to this person/company, not a generic hook.
6. **Action-oriented conclusions.** Every piece ends with a clear next step: a question inviting response, or a direct command. Never a summary.
7. **Signature consistency.** Same sign-off across channels. Builds familiarity.
8. **Formal address for new contacts.** Shift to informal only after rapport is established.
9. **Contrast-driven tension.** The best hooks pair a negative statement with a positive metric: "Most investors fail at X. We have 0 failures."
10. **Value hints, not full disclosure.** Tease what's possible; don't deliver everything in the first message.

## Platform-Specific Execution

### Social Media (Instagram, LinkedIn)

**Hook structure:**
- Emotional contrast: "[negative statement] + [positive metric]"
- Bold claim backed by a number
- Pattern interrupt (counterintuitive fact)

**Post structure:**
1. Hook (emotional contrast or number)
2. Problem statement
3. Insight or mechanism
4. Proof (specific numbers, not vague claims)
5. CTA — question inviting comments OR direct command

**Carousel arc (7 slides):**
- Slide 1: shock/hook (max 18 words)
- Slides 2-5: problem → mechanism → proof → application
- Slide 6: transformation/result
- Slide 7: urgency/CTA

**LinkedIn:** 1,200-1,500 characters. Open with a surprising statistic or contrarian position. Develop across 3-4 short paragraphs. End with an engagement question.

**Instagram:** carousels and reels outperform static posts. Algorithm in 2026 weights Shares > Saves > Comments > Likes. Create "I need to send this" content.

### Cold Email

Format:
```
Subject: [number or question]
P1 (40 words): personalization rationale — why THIS person, why NOW
P2 (80 words): context + value proposition with a specific number
P3 (40 words): social proof reference (one specific data point)
CTA: action verb + clear benefit
Sign-off: [First name only]
```

Rules:
- Max 150 words.
- Subject line: specific number OR specific question, never generic.
- Follow-up: reference context from prior message before introducing anything new.
- Formal address until rapport is established.

### Pitch Materials

- Open with metrics, not narrative: "47M revenue, 0 failures, 3-year track record."
- Compress the problem to one sentence.
- Zero corporate buzzwords (see Forbidden Language below).
- Every slide/section ends with a number or a named next action.

## Forbidden Language

### Corporate Jargon (banned)
- "innovative," "revolutionary," "synergy," "disruptive," "cutting-edge"
- Any superlative without a number to back it up.

### Weak Hedges (banned)
- "possibly," "perhaps," "maybe," "I dare say," "if I may," "it seems that"
- "I'm reaching out because..." — just state the point.

### AI-Sounding Constructions (banned)
- "In today's world..."
- "It goes without saying..."
- "It is important to note..."
- Perfectly balanced lists (3 items of exactly equal weight look generated)
- Uniform sentence lengths (vary short/medium/long)

## How to Build a Style Clone From Scratch

This is the methodology for extracting and encoding any person's voice:

### Step 1: Corpus Collection
- Collect 50-100+ samples of natural writing: messages, emails, social posts, voice notes transcribed.
- Include context labels: platform, audience, intent (pitch vs. casual vs. urgent).
- Source diversity matters — voice shifts by context. Capture all contexts.

### Step 2: Pattern Extraction (what to look for)
- Average sentence length and maximum sentence length.
- Vocabulary fingerprint: what words appear frequently that are unusual? What words are conspicuously absent?
- Opener patterns: how does this person start messages? Do they use pleasantries or dive in?
- Closer patterns: how do they end? Question, command, or summary?
- Number usage: do they quantify everything or use qualitative language?
- Hedging frequency: confident statements or lots of qualifications?
- Paragraph structure: one idea per paragraph? Long blocks?

### Step 3: Build the Rules Document
- Positive rules: "Do X" (e.g., "Open with a number when making a claim")
- Negative rules: "Never do Y" (e.g., "Never use 'innovative'")
- Platform-specific variations: same voice, different register

### Step 4: Build the Forbidden Word List
- Pull every instance of language the person edits out of their own drafts.
- Pull every word that feels "off" when you read it back in their voice.
- Categories: hedges, jargon, AI tells, filler openers.

### Step 5: Pre-Send Quality Gate
Verify before publishing:
- [ ] No forbidden vocabulary
- [ ] At least one specific number (if making a claim)
- [ ] Ends with action, not summary
- [ ] Sentence lengths vary (no robotic uniformity)
- [ ] Personalized opening (for outreach)
- [ ] Would a human write this? (AI tells removed)

## The Anatomy of a High-Performing Hook

**Curiosity Gap:** "What most [audience] don't know about [topic]"
**Relatable Pain:** "You're leaving [specific amount] on the table."
**Pattern Interrupt:** "Bad news: [counterintuitive fact]"
**Urgent Command:** "Save this — you'll need it."

The best hooks do exactly one of these. They don't combine them.

## Content Repurposing Workflow

One pillar piece → 10+ formats:

```
PILLAR (60-90 min video OR 2000+ words)
├── VIDEO (YouTube/long-form)
├── PODCAST (audio strip)
├── REELS (4-6 key moments, 30-60 sec each)
├── CAROUSEL (3-5 data-driven carousels)
├── SINGLE POST (1 striking insight)
├── NEWSLETTER (300-500 words)
├── BLOG POST (SEO, 1000+ words)
├── STORIES (polls, questions, quotes)
├── LINKEDIN (B2B tone, 200-300 words)
└── PDF/CHECKLIST (lead magnet)
```

Efficiency: batch repurposing (1× per week, all formats from 1 pillar) produces 300% more engagement at 60% less time than creating each format independently.

## Email Sequence Structure (nurture)

80/20 rule: 80% value, 20% pitch.
One email = one topic + one CTA.
150-300 words, mobile-first.

6-email welcome sequence:
| # | Day | Content | CTA |
|---|-----|---------|-----|
| 1 | Immediately | Who you are + what they can expect | "Read [story]" |
| 2 | D+3 | Education: mechanism behind the thing you do | "Download guide" |
| 3 | D+7 | Social proof: specific results, specific clients | "See results" |
| 4 | D+10 | Objections addressed: risk, timeline, complexity | "FAQ" |
| 5 | D+14 | Soft CTA: consultation or calculator | "Calculate [outcome]" |
| 6 | D+21 | Hard CTA: limited capacity or deadline | "Reserve spot" |
