## 1. Headline Laws

### Ogilvy's Law (David Ogilvy, Confessions of an Advertising Man, 1963)
- Headlines are read by 5x more people than body copy. The headline is the ad.
- Every headline must answer: "What is in this for me?" from the reader's perspective.
- Specificity beats vagueness: "How to Win Friends" (vague) vs. "How to Win Friends and Influence People" (complete promise).
- News value increases recall by 22%. "New," "Introducing," "Announcing," "Now" (when genuine) all boost attention.
- Forbidden without proof: "best," "superior," "unique," "world-class." Every claim needs substantiation in the body.

### Sugarman's Slippery Slide
The sole purpose of the headline is to get the reader to read the first sentence.
The sole purpose of the first sentence is to get the reader to read the second sentence.
Every element creates downward momentum. Nothing stops the slide.

**Practical application:**
- Headline: Create a gap. "Why most ads fail on the first line."
- First sentence: Must be so short or compelling it can't be skipped. One or two syllables. "It's simple."
- Second sentence: Slightly longer, payoff starts. Now reader is committed.
- Third sentence onward: They're sliding. Deliver.

### Hormozi Value Equation Maximization
```
Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Probability) ÷ (Time Delay × Effort)
```
Apply to every headline:
- Maximize Dream Outcome: be specific about what they get. "4.8x ROAS" > "better results."
- Maximize Perceived Probability: include proof. "Used by 12,400 brands" > "proven method."
- Minimize Time Delay: "in 30 days" > "eventually." Speed of result is a headline variable.
- Minimize Effort: "without hiring an agency" / "in 20 minutes" / "copy-paste system."

**Headline formula derived from value equation:**
"[Specific result] in [short time] without [biggest feared effort/sacrifice]"
Example: "Cut your cost-per-lead by 40% in 30 days — without increasing your ad budget."
Example: "4.8x ROAS on Meta in 6 weeks — without an agency or media buyer."

### Rule of One (Gary Halbert)
One reader. One big idea. One dominant emotion. One CTA.
Headlines that try to say two things say nothing. Every word that doesn't serve the one idea must go.

## 2. Copy Formulas — Filled Examples

### PAS — Problem, Agitate, Solve
Best for: social ads primary text, email, landing page sections

**Template:**
```
P: [State the specific problem — make it recognizable]
A: [Make the problem feel worse, more urgent, more expensive]
S: [Introduce the product as the clear resolution + CTA]
```

**Example (e-commerce ad, Meta primary text):**
```
Your Meta ROAS dropped below 2x and you don't know why.

Every day you run the same creative, frequency climbs, CPMs rise,
and the algorithm optimizes toward the wrong signals. The longer
you wait, the more expensive it gets to fix — because burned
audiences don't come back.

The Creative Rotation System: 3 fresh angles per week, automatic
kill rules by day 4, sustained ROAS above 3.5x. [See how it works]
```

**Example (Czech B2B, LinkedIn primary text):**
```
Váš obchodní tým posílá emaily. Nikdo neodpovídá.

Průměrná míra odpovědí na studené emaily v CZ B2B je pod 2 %.
Každý kontakt, který nereaguje, je obchodní příležitost, která
zmizí — a vaši konkurenti ji obsadí.

Sekvence 5 emailů, která dosahuje 40 % open rate a 8 % reply rate.
Bez falešné urgence. Bez generických šablon. [Stáhnout šablonu]
```

### AIDA — Attention, Interest, Desire, Action
Best for: full-page ads, email sequences, landing pages

**Template:**
```
A: [Interrupt attention — bold claim, surprising stat, or provocative question]
I: [Why this matters to them specifically — mechanism, problem context]
D: [Evidence of desire: proof, testimonials, case study, specifics]
A: [Single CTA — one action, no options]
```

```
   I've seen that doesn't overpromise." — Jan K., Founder, NovaBrands.cz

A: Apply for the next cohort — 8 spots, Q3 opens Monday. [Apply now]
```

### BAB — Before, After, Bridge
Best for: social proof ads, testimonials, email subject lines

**Template:**
```
BEFORE: [Describe the painful current state in vivid, specific terms]
AFTER: [Describe the desired future state — specific, measurable]
BRIDGE: [Your product is the path from before to after]
```

**Example (Google Ads — Performance Max):**
```
BEFORE: Running Google PMax with one asset group, wondering why it burns
        budget on irrelevant search terms while Shopping underperforms.

AFTER:  Three separated asset groups by product category, brand excluded
        from PMax. Shopping 40% more efficient. CPL down 28% in 90 days.

BRIDGE: The structure change takes 45 minutes to implement. Here's exactly
        what to do — and what NOT to do when restructuring an active
        PMax campaign without resetting the algorithm. [Get the guide]
```

### 4Ps — Picture, Promise, Prove, Push
Best for: direct mail, landing pages, VSL scripts

**Template:**
```
PICTURE: [Paint a vivid scene of the desired outcome]
PROMISE: [State what you deliver — specific, believable]
PROVE: [Evidence: case study, data, testimonials with numbers]
PUSH: [CTA with urgency or scarcity]
```

**Example (Czech investment product):**
```
PICTURE: Otevřete e-mailovou schránku v pondělí ráno. Výpis z portfolia
         ukazuje 8,2% roční výnos. Bez výkyvů akciového trhu. Bez starostí.

         Fixní výnos 7,5–8,5 % p.a. Splatnost 24 měsíců.

PROVE:   174 investorů. Nula defaultů od roku 2019. Průměrná investice:
         320 000 Kč. "Konečně pasivní příjem, kterému rozumím." — M. Novák,

PUSH:    Emise Q3 2026: kapacita 15 M Kč. Zbývá 4,2 M Kč.
         Uzávěrka přihlášek: 30. dubna. [Rezervovat pozici]
```


Best for: price-justification ads, limited offers, trust-building

**Template:**
```

[CTA]
```

```
3. The price increases in Q3. Q1 price was €2,400. Q2 is €2,800.

   The Q2 price is locked only until Friday.

These are the reasons. [Reserve your spot — 2 remaining]
```

## 3. Hook Formula Library — 15 Named Patterns with Examples

For each pattern: use in first line of primary text, first frame of video, or ad headline.

### 1. Curiosity Gap
Name the category of payoff; withhold the mechanism.
"The one Meta setting 90% of advertisers have never touched — it controls who sees your ad first."
"What a €47,000/day ad account does in the first 3 seconds that yours doesn't."

### 2. Pattern Interrupt
Violate genre expectation visually or verbally.
"Stop. This is not the ad you think it is." [White text, black background in colorful feed]
"I'm going to waste your time for the next 30 seconds. Keep reading."

### 3. Contrarian Claim
Take the opposite position of conventional wisdom.
"Stop A/B testing your Facebook copy. It's not the copy that's killing your ROAS."
"Interest targeting on Meta is almost always worse than going broad. Here's why."

### 4. Problem-Aware Hook
Meet the audience at their known pain point.
"Still getting CPMs above €15 on cold traffic despite good creative? This is why."
"Your Facebook ads look great. Your ROAS doesn't. Here's the actual problem."

### 5. Problem-Unaware Hook
Create awareness of a problem they don't know they have.
"Every time you increase a Meta ad budget by more than 30%, you reset the algorithm. Most brands don't know this."
"Your TikTok ads are being served to the wrong demographic and here's how to tell."

### 6. Contextual / Situational
Reference the exact situation the viewer is likely in.
"If you're scaling a Meta campaign past €5K/day, this one setting matters more than your creative."
"Running Google PMax right now? Your brand terms might be cannibalizing search results."

### 7. Social Proof Hook
Lead with the proof. Data first.
"4.8x ROAS. 30 days. €0 to €45K ad spend. Here's the exact campaign structure."
"174 investors. Zero defaults since 2019. This is what they saw that others missed."

### 8. Urgency Hook
Real time or quantity constraint. Must be verifiable.
"Offer closes Friday at midnight. After that, the price doubles — and we mean it."
"12 spots left for Q3 onboarding. The application takes 4 minutes."

### 9. Status-Elevation Hook
The audience as aspirational; your information as access to the elite tier.
"What top-performing e-commerce brands do with creative testing that others copy 6 months later."
"The way €50M/year businesses think about Meta bidding is completely different from what's being taught."

### 10. Before-After-Bridge Hook
Compression of the transformation into one or two lines.
"January: €8K spend, 0.9x ROAS. March: same budget, 3.8x ROAS. One structural change."
"Couldn't book a single investor meeting. Changed one sentence in the subject line. 3 meetings in a week."

### 11. Scary Truth Hook
Uncomfortable fact the audience doesn't want to hear but needs to.
"Your ad creative isn't the problem. Your landing page is converting at 0.8%. No ad can overcome that."
"The reason your campaigns keep dying after 2 weeks isn't the algorithm. It's your creative volume."

### 12. Surprising Statistic Hook
A number that violates the viewer's assumption.
"85% of Meta video ads are watched with sound off. Most are built for audio."
"3 seconds. That's how long before 60% of viewers skip in-stream YouTube. Does your hook work in 3 seconds?"

### 13. Contrarian Confession
Radical honesty. Admitting something most experts hide.
"I built my agency on interest-based Meta targeting. I was wrong. Broad works better. Here's the data."
"Most cold email 'personalization' is theater. The real driver is the subject line. Here's what actually works."

### 14. Time-Pressure Conditional
Deadline framing that creates FOMO without fake scarcity.
"If you start this before Q3, you'll have your first profitable campaigns running before the holiday season."
"Every week you don't restructure your PMax is a week of budget leaking to irrelevant placements."

### 15. If-Then Qualifier
Selects the right audience through a conditional test.
"If you're spending more than €3K/month on Meta and your ROAS is below 2x — this is specifically for you."
"If you've tried Google Ads, got burned, and never went back — you need to see what changed in 2024."

## 6. Emotion Ladder — Which Emotion Drives Which Buying Motivation

| Emotion | Driver | Copy style | Best for |
|---------|--------|-----------|---------|
| Fear (of loss) | Loss aversion (2x amplifier — Kahneman) | "You're losing X every day you don't..." | High-ticket, competitive markets, urgency |
| Greed | Gain-seeking | Specific ROI numbers, income claims (with proof) | Financial products, business tools |
| Frustration | Pain amplification | Mirror exact language of their daily problem | Problem-aware audiences, retarget |
| Aspiration | Status + identity | "What top performers do..." | E-commerce, personal development |
| Curiosity | Information gap | Withhold mechanism, name the category | Cold traffic, content downloads |
| Trust | Certainty + safety | Proof, numbers, specifics, guarantees | High-ticket, new brand |
| Excitement | Novelty | "New method," "what changed in 2026," first-mover | New product launches |
| Belonging | Tribal identity | "Join X teams who..." community framing | SaaS, communities |

**Decision rule:** Match emotion to audience awareness level.
- Unaware (Level 1): Curiosity or Fear
- Problem-aware (Level 2): Frustration + Hope
- Solution-aware (Level 3): Aspiration + Trust
- Product-aware (Level 4): Trust + Urgency
- Most-aware (Level 5): Greed + Urgency + Belonging

## 7. Power Words and Weak Words

### Power Words (50+)
**Specificity words:** Free, New, Now, Proven, Guaranteed, Exact, Specific, Complete, Full, Immediate
**Trust words:** Verified, Certified, Tested, Documented, Named, Audited, Accredited
**Urgency words:** Today, Deadline, Closing, Final, Last, Expires, Limited, Remaining, Cutoff
**Outcome words:** Results, Revenue, Profit, Growth, Return, Savings, Reduction, Increase, Transformation
**Ease words:** Simple, Quick, Fast, Easy, Automated, Done-for-you, Copy-paste, Without, No more
**Identity words:** Founder, Expert, Top-performing, Elite, Serious, Professional, Leading
**Czech power words:** Zdarma, Okamžitě, Garantovaný, Ověřený, Výsledky, Bez rizika, Zbývá [N] míst

### Weak Words (avoid)
| Weak | Replacement |
|------|-------------|
| "Good" | Specific metric: "4.8x ROAS" |
| "Better" | "2.1x higher ROAS than baseline" |
| "Some" | "14 brands" |
| "Many" | "12,400 teams" |
| "Various" | List them specifically |
| "Solution" | Name what it actually does |
| "Leverage" | "Use," "apply," "run" |
| "Synergy" | Delete |
| "Innovation" | Name the actual innovation |
| "World-class" | Cite the third-party that said it |

## 8. Iteration Framework — Change One Variable

### Test Cycle Structure
**Round 1:** 1 control + 4 hook challengers (same body, same CTA, 4 different hooks)
**Round 2:** Winning hook × 4 body challengers (winning hook, same CTA, 4 body variations)
**Round 3:** Winning hook + body × 4 CTA challengers
**Round 4:** Winning combination × 4 new angle challengers (entirely different creative concepts)

**Impact hierarchy (test in this order):**
1. Angle / concept (biggest impact)
2. Hook (second biggest)
3. Visual / format
4. Body copy
5. CTA (smallest marginal impact)

Never test body copy variations before validating the angle. Optimize in order.

### Kill Threshold
- CTR: 500+ impressions before killing; don't kill in first 48 hours
- CPA: 50+ conversions needed for statistical confidence on cost-per-acquisition signals
- Minimum test budget: 2-3x target CPA per ad before kill decision

### Statistical Significance
- Target: 95% confidence (industry standard)
- For CTR: ~500 impressions per variant
- For CVR/CPA: ~50 conversions per variant
- Rule of thumb: if one variant has 2x+ CTR with 1,000+ impressions, it's the winner without waiting for full statistical significance

### Winner Propagation
After a winner is identified:
1. Scale winner to full budget
2. Create 3-5 variations of the winner's angle (same concept, different execution)
3. Keep running winner while testing variations
4. Never pause winner to test a challenger (run simultaneously)

## 9. Czech-Language Copywriting Considerations

### Register (Formality)
- B2B Czech: vykání mandatory ("Vám," "Váš," "Váš podnik"). Lowercase "vy" = tykání. Capitalize for formality.
- B2C / younger consumer: tykání acceptable for platforms like TikTok and Instagram targeting under-30 audiences
- Mixed in same campaign = brand inconsistency; pick one and commit

### False Cognate Traps (EN→CZ translation errors)
- "Eventually" ≠ "eventuálně" (eventuálně = possibly/perhaps)
- "Actual" ≠ "aktuální" (aktuální = current/up-to-date)
- "Prospect" ≠ "prospekt" (prospekt = brochure)
- "Protect" ≠ "protestovat" (protestovat = to protest)
- Machine-translated Czech copy often contains these; always review with native speaker

### Filip Dopita Vocabulary Rules (CZ brand voice)
Signature phrases that build authenticity: "koukl jsem," "fakt," "klidně," "mrkni," "zajímavý"
Forbidden forever: "synergický," "disruptivní," "implementovat," "leveragovat," "paradigma," "holistický"
Blocked structures: "Dovoluji si Vám napsat," "Doufám, že tento email zastane Vás v dobrém zdraví"
Strong closers: "Stojí to za 5 minut" / "Mrkni a dej vědět" — not summaries, not pleasantries

### Czech-Specific Outreach Subject Line Formulas (Filip Dopita)
- Curiosity gap: "Co většina investorů neví..."
- Relatable pain: "Necháváte peníze na 0,1% účtu?"
- Pattern interrupt: "Špatná zpráva: [protiintuitivní fakt]"
- Word limit: 2-4 slova, lowercase, no punctuation tricks
- Spam signals: "Dovoluji si," "S pozdravom," "Navazuji na předchozí email" as subject line

## 10. Adam Kropáček Batch Generation System

### 3-Wave Creative System
**Wave 1 — Core angles (15-25 ads):**
Generate 3-5 distinct angles, 5 ad variations per angle.
Angles must each address a different motivation driver:
1. Pain point ("Stop wasting €X on...")
2. Outcome ("Achieve Y in Z days")
3. Social proof ("Join N teams who...")
4. Curiosity ("The X secret top [role] use")
5. Contrarian ("Why [common belief] doesn't work")

**Wave 2 — Expand winners (10 ads):**
After 7-14 days: identify top 20% by primary metric.
Generate 10 more variations from winning angles — new hooks, same concept.
Double down on what the data shows works.

**Wave 3 — Wild cards (3-5 ads):**
Completely different angles: emotional, format-shift, contrarian, unexpected.
Some will fail. One may become the new control.

### Quality Filter (remove before publishing)
- Copy longer than platform character limit
- Duplicate headlines that say the same thing differently
- Combinations where headline + description contradict each other
- Policy-risk language: guaranteed, #1, lose weight, make money fast

## 11. Filip Dopita — Email Subject Patterns for Outreach

Cross-applicable to ad headline writing. Patterns that drive opens apply to hooks.

### 5-Email Cold Sequence Subject Line Formulas
| Email | Day | Subject Formula | Max words |
|-------|-----|-----------------|-----------|
| 1 | 0 | Personalization + curiosity gap | 4 words, no punctuation |
| 2 | 3 | Re: {previous_subject} | Thread continuation |
| 3 | 7 | Fresh insight or case study | Number-led |
| 4 | 14 | Direct single question | <5 words |
| 5 | 21 | "I'll let this be" | Break-up framing |

**Subject line rules:**
- NO "Re:" in first email (spam signal)
- Lowercase, no punctuation tricks
- Looks like internal company email
- Email 5 ("I'll let this be" pattern): highest reply rate in Czech B2B context (Filip Dopita data, OneFlow 2026)

### Email Anatomy (Perfect Structure — Filip Dopita)
- Para 1 (40 words): Problem or surprising insight
- Para 2 (80 words): Context + brand perspective
- Para 3 (40 words): Transition to solution
- Single CTA with clear benefit
- Total: under 180 words; under 100 for cold outreach
