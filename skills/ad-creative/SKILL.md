# Ad Creative

Generate and iterate high-performing ad creative for paid advertising platforms.

## Two Core Modes

**Generation Mode** — Create headlines, descriptions, and primary text from scratch based on product context, audience insights, and platform specs.

**Iteration Mode** — Analyze performance data to identify winning patterns, then generate variations that build on top performers while testing unexplored angles.

## Workflow

1. **Define angles** — Establish 3–5 distinct reasons someone would click: pain point, outcome, social proof, curiosity, authority, urgency.
2. **Generate variations** — Multiple options per angle, varying word choice, specificity, tone, and structure.
3. **Validate specs** — Confirm all copy fits platform character limits before handoff.
4. **Organize for upload** — Present in structured format matching platform requirements.

## Platform Character Limits

| Platform | Field | Limit |
|---|---|---|
| Google Ads (RSA) | Headline | 30 characters |
| Google Ads (RSA) | Description | 90 characters |
| Meta | Primary text (visible) | 125 characters |
| Meta | Headline | 40 characters |
| LinkedIn | Intro text | 150 characters |
| LinkedIn | Headline | 70 characters |
| TikTok | Ad text | 80 characters (recommended) |
| Twitter/X | Tweet text | 280 characters |

## Angle Frameworks

### Pain Point Angle
Lead with the problem, imply the fix.
- "Still losing clients to slow follow-up?"
- "Your competitors are running ads. You're not."

### Outcome Angle
Lead with the result the audience wants.
- "Cut your cost-per-lead by 40% in 30 days."
- "Book 3x more demos without hiring another SDR."

### Social Proof Angle
Numbers, testimonials, recognizable names.
- "10,000 teams use this to close faster."
- "Rated #1 by G2 for 3 years running."

### Curiosity Angle
Create an information gap.
- "The targeting trick most media buyers ignore."
- "Why your ROAS drops after day 7 — and how to fix it."

### Urgency / Scarcity Angle
Time or quantity constraint.
- "Offer ends Friday. Lock in your rate."
- "Only 12 spots left for Q2 onboarding."

## Quality Standards

**Strong headlines:**
- Specific, not vague ("Save 4 hours/week" not "Save time").
- Benefit-focused, not feature-focused.
- Active voice.
- Include numbers when possible.

**Strong descriptions:**
- Complement the headline — add proof, handle an objection, or reinforce the CTA.
- Do not repeat headline content.
- End with a clear action: "Start free trial," "Get your report," "Book a call."

## Iteration Protocol

When performance data is available:

1. Identify the top 20% of creative by primary metric (CTR, CVR, CPA).
2. Extract patterns: angle type, opening word, sentence structure, tone.
3. Generate 5–10 new variants that replicate winning patterns while testing one new variable per variant.
4. Retire bottom 20% after statistically significant data (min. 500 impressions per ad for CTR; 50 conversions for CVR/CPA).

## Common Mistakes

- Writing headlines that only make sense together (each RSA headline must stand alone).
- Ignoring character limits — copy gets truncated, CTA disappears.
- Iterating without performance data — gut-feel "improvements" regress winners.
- Testing too many variables at once — isolate one change per variant.
