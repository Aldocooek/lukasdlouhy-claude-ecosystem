## Core Principles

**Clarity > Cleverness.** Clear beats creative.
**Benefits > Features.** "Cut reporting from 4h to 15min" not "automated reports."
**Specificity > Vagueness.** "$13K deal" not "big results." "36K Kč/měsíc" not "serious income."
**Customer language.** Mirror words from reviews, calls, support tickets.
**One idea per section.** Build a logical argument top to bottom.

---

## Writing Style

1. Simple: "use" not "utilize," "help" not "facilitate"
2. Specific: no "streamline," "optimize," "innovative" without numbers
3. Active: "we generate reports" not "reports are generated"
4. Confident: remove "almost," "very," "really," "might"
5. Show: describe outcome, don't adjectivize
6. Honest: fabricated stats erode trust + create liability

## Jeremy's Banned-Words List

Never write these in copy. Each one either signals pressure, implies prior deception, or triggers nervous-system resistance.

**Pressure/authority triggers (delete on sight):**
- "You need to" — prescriptive, triggers pushback
- "I think you should" — same
- "This is the best" — pressure signal
- "You must" / "You have to" — authority triggers

**Credibility-eroding phrases:**
- "Honestly" — implies you were lying before
- "To be transparent" / "Let me be honest with you" — same
- "I'm going to be real with you" — telegraphs that previous wasn't real

**Self-focused language:**
- "What I do is…" — prospect doesn't care
- "We are pleased to offer…" — corporate vacuum

**Permission-asking signals:**
- "Can I ask you a question?" — telegraphs interrogation
- "Would it be okay if I…" — lowers your authority frame

**Replace all of the above with:**
- "Out of curiosity…" → opens curiosity gap, no resistance
- "Help me understand…" → inverts power, they do the work
- "I'm not sure this even makes sense for you…" → paradox of detachment builds trust
- "It sounds like…" / "What I'm hearing is…" → reflective, not prescriptive
- "I might be wrong, but…" → disarms before challenging

## NEPQ Question Architecture in Copy

**Core premise (Jeremy Miner):** The brain resists being sold, not its own conclusions. Copy that makes readers articulate their own pain + desired outcome converts at 2-3x the rate of copy that states it for them.

**Translation rule:** Convert NEPQ question stages into copy section types.

| NEPQ Stage | Copy Section |
|---|---|
| Situation questions | "Does this sound familiar?" section |
| Problem awareness | "The real problem" / pain block |
| Consequence questions | "What happens if nothing changes" |
| Solution awareness | "What actually works" — let reader arrive |
| Commitment | CTA framing that asks "what's holding you back?" |

### Copy-Ready NEPQ Question Sequences

**Consequence sequence (embed in pain section):**
> "How is this affecting you beyond just [surface symptom]?
> What's the thing about this problem that bothers you the most?
> If you don't change this in the next 90 days, what does that look like?"

Each question is rhetorical in copy — reader answers internally. That internal answer IS the conviction.

**Desire sequence (embed before solution reveal):**
> "What would the ideal outcome look like if this was fully solved?
> What does life look like for you when [problem] is gone?
> If you could wave a magic wand, what's the one thing that changes immediately?"

**Curiosity gap opener (for ads/email subject lines):**
- "Out of curiosity — [common mistake they're making]?"
- "I'm not sure if this even applies to you, but [specific insight]."
- "Help me understand — why would you [status quo they're stuck in]?"

**Smoke screen test (embed in pricing section):**
> "If investment wasn't a consideration, would this be the direction you'd go?"
> Yes → investment is a smoke screen. Reframe it as cost of inaction.
> (The reader does this math themselves — copy just sets up the question.)

## Tristan's Call-Review-to-Copy Translation

Tristan's call analysis framework: 5 markers he looks for when reviewing sales calls. Each maps to a copy failure mode.

| Call failure (what closers do wrong) | Copy equivalent | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Let vague answers pass without probing | Vague benefit bullets ("grow your business") | Specifify: "revenue by $X in Y days" |
| Didn't challenge at least once in discovery | Only validation copy — no cognitive dissonance | Add one brutal truth section before solution |
| Never tested commitment before close | No pre-CTA qualification layer | Add "this is right for you if…" gate |
| Didn't hold frame under pressure | Copy folds when it anticipates objections (excessive disclaimers) | State terms, don't hedge |
| Didn't use silence after close question | Wall of text at CTA, no white space | Use deliberate line breaks; let the close breathe |

**Tristan's key insight for copy:** Rapport creates comfort. Challenging creates cognitive dissonance. Most copy only does rapport — validates the reader's worldview. One challenging section (a brutal truth, a reframe, a "you might be wrong about this") creates the gap that makes people move.

**Challenge section template:**
> "Here's what most [persona] get wrong about [topic]:
> [State their current assumption]
> Interesting — most people who've been at that stage for [X months] find out later they were working on the wrong problem.
> Is that possible here?"

## Future Self Framing for CTAs

**Source:** Jeremy Miner NEPQ future-self close. Works in copy because it gets readers to articulate their own ROI before you do.

**Core mechanic:** Project them 90 days forward having already made the decision. They sell themselves.

**CTA templates:**

**Version 1 — Vision pull:**
> "If you look back 90 days from now, having moved forward on this — what does that change for you?
> That's the question we help you answer.
> [CTA: Book the Call / Start Now]"

**Version 2 — Pain contrast:**
> "Two versions of you. 90 days from now.
> One moved forward. One saved this page and came back to 'think about it.'
> You already know which one you want to be.
> [CTA]"

**Version 3 — Date anchor (Anas):**
> "You're heading into [Q4 / busy season / January]. What happens if you head into that WITHOUT having solved [X]?
> That's 90 days away. This call takes 30 minutes.
> [CTA]"

**Rule:** Never use 2-year consequences in future-self CTAs. Anchor within 30–90 days. Real window, real cost, real decision.

## Per-Persona Copy Flavor Guide

Match copy style to the product, offer, and buyer. Each persona is a distinct tonality register.

### Andres Contreras — Authoritative / Conviction
**Baseline:** Certain, declarative, no hedging. Closes hard. Works from identity.
**Signature moves:** Binary framing ("two types of people"), identity stakes ("think as the person who already has $1M"), consequence reframes.
**Use when:** High-ticket offer with clear transformation; buyer is entrepreneur, ambitious, identity-driven; product is coaching, sales training, high-performance.
**Copy markers:** Short declarative sentences. "$30K/month" not "significant income." Identity labels ("closers," "the exceptional few"). Rhetorical questions that answer themselves.
**Example:**
> "There are two types of business owners.
> One sees the price. One sees the cost of NOT having this.
> One of them is in the same spot two years from now.
> Which one are you?"

### Jeremy Miner — Skeptical-Curious / Interrogative
**Baseline:** Slow, measured, slightly unsure — sounds like he might walk away. Disarms defense before it builds.
**Signature moves:** NEPQ openers ("out of curiosity…"), "I'm not sure this makes sense for you," future-self questions, smoke screen tests.
**Use when:** Prospect has been burned before; offer is premium and trust is the barrier (not price); buyer is analytical, has tried competitors, needs to feel unchased.
**Copy markers:** Questions that don't answer themselves. White space after key lines. Phrases that qualify the reader out ("this isn't for everyone"). No declarative pressure.
**Example:**
> "I'm not sure this is for you.
> Out of curiosity — what's it costing you to stay where you are right now?
> Not in money. In [relationships / sleep / opportunity].
> If the answer is 'a lot' — that's what we work on. If not, we're probably not the right fit."

### Tristan Steckler — Analytical / Challenging
**Baseline:** Deliberate, measured. Comfortable with silence and cognitive dissonance.
**Signature moves:** Challenging the reader's current assumption, smoke screen tests, vague-answer probes, frame holds.
**Use when:** Educated buyer who's done their research and thinks they know what they need; sophisticated B2B; premium consulting or advisory; buyer responds to "someone who gets it."
**Copy markers:** One brutal reframe per page. Specific data points. Qualifying gates. "Interesting — most [personas] at your stage find out later they were solving the wrong problem."
**Example:**
> "If price wasn't a factor, would this be the direction you'd go?
> If yes — then price isn't the real hesitation.
> Something else is. And we should probably talk about that.
> [CTA: Let's figure out what's actually stopping you]"

### Anas NBC — Warm / Energetic / Pattern Interrupt
**Baseline:** Conversational, fast, slightly playful. Energy doesn't drop when there's friction.
**Signature moves:** Familiar greeting interrupts, date anchors, credibility drops early, belief surfacing ("what do you need to be true to say yes?").
**Use when:** Outbound campaigns; cold email/ads where you need to break pattern immediately; entrepreneur-type buyer who responds to energy and peer tone.
**Copy markers:** Unexpected opener. Quick credibility frame. Direct bridge to their world. Warmth throughout.
**Example:**
> "Okay, I wasn't going to send this email.
> But after [X years / X clients], I've seen too many [persona] waste [specific resource] on [wrong thing].
> Here's what we found actually works — and why the people who figured it out first are now [outcome].
> Is that something you're dealing with?"

## CZ/SK: Hormozi-Andres Direct vs Jeremy Interrogative

**Decision matrix — when to use which style in Czech:**

| Signal | Style | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Ad traffic (cold) | Jeremy interrogative | Czechs have BS detectors; skeptical opener lowers guard faster |
| Warm list / email subscribers | Hormozi-Andres direct | They already know you; authority copy converts |
| B2C coaching / personal dev | Mix: Jeremy discovery → Andres close | Discovery builds trust; authority close drives decision |
| B2B / corporate | Jeremy (softer) + Usman data framing | Czech B2B = low tolerance for motivational energy |
| High-ticket (>50K Kč) | Jeremy discovery → Tristan challenge → Andres binary close | Trust → dissonance → identity decision |
| E-commerce / DTC | Andres direct (shorter) | Lower stakes, faster decision, energy works |
| Re-engagement (burned before) | Jeremy 100% | "What is it about this time you're hoping would be different?" |

**Czech-specific notes:**
- Czechs read interrogative copy as "this person is honest, not pushy." Direct Hormozi copy can read as American hype without proof points.
- Andres binary framing ("Jsou dva typy lidí...") works well in Czech — but MUST follow immediately with a specific number or Czech readers disengage.
- Jeremy's "out of curiosity" → Czech equivalent: "Jen ze zvědavosti…" or "Mimochodem…" — both land naturally.
- Tristan's challenging tone → in Czech, soften slightly: "Zajímavé — většina [persona] v podobné situaci zjistila…" vs "you're probably wrong about this."
- Never consequence in years for Czech audience. 30–90 day window is the maximum psychological reach that lands as real.

**Czech interrogative hook examples:**
> "Jen ze zvědavosti — co tě stojí zůstat tam, kde teď jsi? Ne v penězích. V [čase / příležitosti / vztazích]."

> "Nevím jestli to pro tebe má smysl. Ale pokud řešíš [problém] déle než 6 měsíců — stojí za to si o tom 20 minut popovídat."

> "Co by muselo být pravda, abys řekl/a ano — ještě dnes?"

## Objection Pre-Handling in Copy

**Rule:** Embed reframes BEFORE price reveal. Never let price land in a vacuum.

### The Pre-Handle Structure
```
1. Acknowledge the objection as a future objection
2. Flip the frame before they raise it
3. Only then reveal price
```

**Example sequence (for high-ticket offer page):**
> "Before you see the investment: most people who look at this page are thinking 'expensive compared to what?' That's the right question.
>
> Expensive compared to what you have in the bank? Sure.
> Expensive compared to $36K/month you're losing to bad closes? No.
>
> The investment is [PRICE]."

### Core Objection Reframes (copy-ready)

**"Too expensive"**
> "When you say it's too expensive — compared to what? Staying where you are, or something else? Because staying here has a price too. It's just not on an invoice."

**"Need to think about it"**
> "'I need to think about it' means you haven't yet seen the full cost of staying where you are. Let's fix that."

**"Need my partner's buy-in"**
> "Heavy is the head that wears the crown — not because of the crown, but because of the decisions you make when you wear it. This decision is yours to make."

**"Been burned before"**
> "What is it about this time that you're hoping would be different? If you can answer that — you're already past where you were last time."

**"Need a guarantee"**
> "There are two types of people. The first says 'I need a guarantee.' The second says 'I'm going to become the guarantee.' One of them becomes successful. Which one?"

**"Not the right time"**
> "You're heading into [season / Q4 / January]. What happens if you head into that WITHOUT having solved this? That's 90 days away. This call takes 30 minutes."

**"Can get it cheaper elsewhere"**
> "Lower numbers usually mean something's been removed. If both cost exactly the same, which would you choose? Then we know it's not about price."

**"I don't have the money"**
> "The money isn't the resource you're lacking. Resourcefulness is. The question is whether you're the type of person who finds a way when something matters enough."

## Proof Stack Framework

**Hierarchy (specific > vague, always):**

| Weak | Strong |
|------|--------|
| "Big results" | "$13K deal closed in 4 minutes" |
| "Our clients do well" | "5 closers, $30K/month each" |
| "Significant income" | "$65K/month profit at age 17" |
| "Many testimonials" | "Hundreds went through the program with the same resources" |
| "Proven system" | "10% improvement/month compounds to 300%+ over a year" |

**Proof Stack Template:**
```
CLAIM (specific outcome)
↓
MECHANISM (why/how it works)
↓
EVIDENCE ($ amount, timeframe, or named metric)
↓
TRANSFER (how this applies to the reader)
```

**CZ/SK specifics:**
- Use Kč amounts wherever possible: "ztratíte 36 000 Kč/měsíc" not "significant monthly losses"
- Localize timeframes: "za 90 dní" not "a quarter"
- Localize credibility signals: Czech/Slovak case study > US one, always

**Template:**

There are two types of [PERSON].

## Tonality Rules

**The 4 tonalities (master all for copy too):** Casual, Curious, Skeptical, Concerned.

- **Casual** = peer, not guru. "Look, here's the thing:" not "We are pleased to announce."
- **Curious** = questions that make them search their own memory. "When did you last close a deal you were proud of?"
- **Skeptical** = pre-handle your own claims. "I know this sounds like every other offer. Here's what's different."
- **Concerned** = downward inflection in text. "The reality is, most people who see this will do nothing. I'd rather you be angry at this email than comfortable with your current situation."

**Short sentences win.** One idea. Full stop. Next sentence.

**No corporate hedging.** Delete: "might," "could potentially," "we believe," "in some cases," "we strive to." Replace with facts or cut.

**Pair brutal truth with solution.** Never land on pain without a bridge. "You're losing $36K/month to objections. Here's the 4-stage script that closes them."

**Silence as power.** White space after a key claim = the reader's internal pause. Use line breaks deliberately.

## Page Structure

### Above the Fold
- **Headline:** single most important message. Specific > generic.
- **Subheadline:** expands + adds specificity. 1-2 sentences.
- **Primary CTA:** [Action Verb] + [What They Get] + [Qualifier]

**Headline formulas:**
- "{Achieve outcome} without {pain point}"
- "The {category} for {specific person}"
- "Never {unpleasant event} again"
- "Two types of [person]: which one are you?"
- "What [timeframe] of [action] does to [metric]"
- "Stop [losing X]. Start [gaining Y]."
- "Out of curiosity — [question that makes them search their experience]?" *(Jeremy style)*

### Core Section Sequence

| Section | Purpose | High-Ticket Addition |
|---------|---------|---------------------|
| Social Proof | Logos, stats, testimonials | Specific $ amounts, timeframes |
| Problem/Pain | Show you understand | Consequence questions: "two days, two weeks, two months, two years" |
| Brutal Truth (optional) | Tristan's challenge — one reframe | "Most [persona] find out later they were working on the wrong problem" |
| Pre-handle Objections | Embed before price | Use binary framing: "two types of buyers" |
| Solution/Benefits | Outcomes (3-5) | Mechanism + evidence per benefit |
| Proof Stack | Case studies | $ deal sizes, not vague claims |
| Future Self CTA | Vision pull or pain contrast | 90-day anchor, not 2-year |

### CTA Copy

**Weak (avoid):** Submit, Sign Up, Learn More, Click Here, Get Started

**Strong:** Start [Specific Thing], Get [Outcome], See [Product] in Action, Book My [Call/Audit/Session]

**Future-self CTA format:**
> "If you look back 90 days from now, having moved forward — what does that change for you?
> [BUTTON: Find Out]"

**Binary CTA format:**
> "You already know if this is for you. The people who book the call — they're already 90 days ahead of the ones who didn't.
> [BUTTON: Book the Call]
> Or keep doing what's working. [BUTTON: Maybe later] (we both know how that ends)"

## Copy Audit Checklist

### Hook Test (3-Second Rule)
- [ ] Does the headline pass the "so what?" test in 3 seconds?
- [ ] Is there a specific number, name, or outcome in the first line?
- [ ] Would a stranger scrolling at speed stop for this?
- [ ] Does it use one of the 4 hook types: curiosity / status / fear-of-loss / pattern interrupt?
- [ ] Is it free of Jeremy's banned words list?
- [ ] Is it free of corporate language ("solutions," "innovative," "streamlined")?

### Proof Test
- [ ] Every claim has a specific metric ($ amount, %, timeframe, named result)?
- [ ] No vague phrases: "big results," "many clients," "significantly improved"?
- [ ] Is there a named case study or specific deal size?
- [ ] Does proof follow the CLAIM → MECHANISM → EVIDENCE → TRANSFER stack?

### CTA Test
- [ ] Is there ONE primary CTA? (two primary CTAs = zero primary CTAs)
- [ ] Does the CTA communicate what they GET, not what they DO?
- [ ] Is future-self framing present near the final CTA? (90-day anchor, not 2-year)
- [ ] Is there a binary framing close near the final CTA?
- [ ] Is urgency present without being manufactured/fake?

### Objection Test
- [ ] Are the top 3 objections addressed BEFORE the price is revealed?
- [ ] Is the price comparison flipped (what they're losing, not what they're spending)?
- [ ] Is there a "two types of people" closer near the CTA?
- [ ] Is the "need to think about it" objection pre-handled?

### Tonality Test
- [ ] Zero hedging language ("might," "could potentially," "we strive")?
- [ ] Zero Jeremy-banned words ("honestly," "to be transparent," "I think you should")?
- [ ] Are sentences short? (avg under 15 words)
- [ ] Is there at least one brutal truth paired with a solution?
- [ ] Is there at least one interrogative/NEPQ-style question the reader answers internally?
- [ ] Does it read like a peer, not a salesperson or press release?

### Persona Fit Test
- [ ] Is the tone matched to the right persona (Andres authority vs Jeremy interrogative vs Tristan challenge vs Anas warm)?
- [ ] For CZ/SK: is the style choice deliberate for THIS audience (see decision matrix above)?
- [ ] Are consequences anchored within 30-90 days, not 2 years?

### CZ/SK Test (if applicable)
- [ ] Correct register: ty/vy chosen deliberately for this audience?
- [ ] Amounts in Kč, not $ (unless international audience)?
- [ ] Czech/Slovak case study present or localized proof?
- [ ] English slang used appropriately (sales, closer, mindset = OK; everything else = check)?

## v3 Additions [v3: jeremy]

### Seeding Doubt via Tonality in Copy (Patch 4)
State a neutral-to-mild positive about their current situation, then ask a leading question in skeptical-concerned tone. Applied to copy as a named technique:

Headline pattern: "[Neutral statement about current solution]. [Skeptical question that implies a problem]."

Examples:
- "You've been advertising on Indeed for 3 years. Do you like the hires you've been getting?"
- "That's a fairly decent coverage. How often does a family your size actually use all of it?"

**The 100% Pattern for copy:**
When targeting people who think they're fine:
> "I mean, it sounds like things are 100% perfect for you. What would you change if you could?"
Copy application: Use "what if you could [do X better]?" framing for audiences who don't self-identify as having a problem. They'll engage because humans never like 100% of anything.

### Hopium Anti-Pattern for Landing Pages (Patch 5)
> "If your presentation is more than 10% of your entire sales process, that's the problem."

Landing page corollary: If your page is 80% features/benefits and 10% consequence/pain, it's a Hopium page. Rebalance: 85% problem articulation + consequence, 10% solution, 5% CTA.

**Problem Finder framing:**
> "Problem solving comes AFTER they purchase. Problem finding is BEFORE."
Copy that finds the problem (surfaces it, makes it real) converts better than copy that solves it. Name the problem on the page; don't race to the solution.

## MODULE: BRAND VOICE & CONSISTENCY (from $100M Branding, 2025)

### Principle
**Every piece of copy is a brand pairing.** You're pairing your business with what you say, how you say it, and what you promise.

Good copy = good brand pairing → ideal customer thinks "this is for me"
Bad copy = bad brand pairing → wrong audience clicks OR right audience repelled

### The 3 Consistency Axes

**1. Avatar Fit**
- Does the language match how your ideal customer talks?
- Are cultural references on-brand?
- Tone match: formal, casual, hip, technical?

**2. Promise Consistency**
- Does this copy promise what we can deliver?
- Does it align with other promises we've made?
- Conflict with existing guarantees or positioning?

**3. Voice Match**
- Sentence rhythm (short/long mix)
- Vocabulary (premium vs accessible)
- Personality (warm/aggressive/authoritative)
- POV (I, we, you)

### Pre-Publish Checklist (5-Point)
Before any piece of copy goes live:
1. ☐ **Avatar language match** — would target person use these words?
2. ☐ **Promise consistency** — matches existing brand promises?
3. ☐ **Voice match** — sounds like us, not like template?
4. ☐ **Proof placement** — testimonials/data where appropriate?
5. ☐ **CTA aligned with brand position** — premium brand = premium CTA, not "bargain"

If 2+ fail → revise. Consistency > cleverness.

### Brand-Aligned Headlines
- **Premium brand** headlines: benefit + status ("Executives Who Travel More Than 20 Days/Month Swear By This")
- **Mass-market brand** headlines: savings + accessibility ("Save 40% on Your Next...")
- **Authority brand** headlines: expertise + results ("The 7-Step Method I Use to...")
- **Community brand** headlines: belonging + identity ("For Anyone Who's Ever...")

Wrong headline style for brand = bouquet mismatch → repels ideal customer.

### Content That Builds Brand
1. **Stories** (recent past, far past) — shows who you are
2. **Proof** (testimonials, case studies) — what others say
3. **Frameworks** (teach your method) — authority positioning
4. **Behind-the-scenes** (process, team) — humanizes brand
5. **Opinions** (takes, hot takes) — attracts similar values

### Content That Breaks Brand
1. Off-avatar humor (alienates target)
2. Jarring tone shifts (breaks voice)
3. Off-promise topics (confuses positioning)
4. Controversy without purpose (liability)
5. Copy-paste templates without customization (generic)

### 3 Red Flags Before Publishing
1. **"It's funny but..."** — if it needs defending, kill it
2. **"Everyone's doing this..."** — if generic, won't differentiate
3. **"We can always delete..."** — assume it lives forever (screenshots)

### Brand Bouquet Expansion Rule
When copy pivots broader (new audience) or narrower (niche):
- Calculate net gain/loss of existing audience
- Kill the variant if engagement drops >25% on existing audience
- Keep if new audience engagement > lost audience (measure!)

### Recovery Copy (Brand Mistake)
If you publish something that backfires:
- **Don't delete it** (increases attention — Streisand effect)
- **Over-publish good stuff** — push bad pairing down feed
- **Double down on positioning** — reinforce who you ARE
- Timeline: 30-60 days of concentrated good content to rebalance

### Mantras
1. "Consistency > cleverness."
2. "Kopíruj bouquet, ne content."
3. "Brand compounds. Every piece of copy is a deposit or withdrawal."
4. "When in doubt, match the bouquet."

## MODULE: HOOK MASTERY (from $100M Goated Ads + Hooks, 2025)

### Core Rule
**80% of effort goes into the first 3-5 seconds (the hook).**
If no one reads/watches past the hook, nothing else matters. Invest accordingly.

### 2 Components of Every Hook
1. **Call Out** — gets attention ("this is for me")
2. **Condition for Value** — promises reward if they consume

### Types of Verbal Hooks
- **Labels:** "Local business owners, I have a gift for you"
- **Yes Questions:** "Would you pay $1,000 to have the business of your dreams?"

## MODULE: OFFER VARIANTS COPY (from $100M Money Models, 2025)

### Why This Matters
Every offer type requires DIFFERENT copy angle. Generic "buy our stuff" copy = low conversion. Match copy to offer type.

### Attraction Offer Copy Patterns

#### Win Your Money Back
Headline templates:
- "Get Your $X Back If [Result] Doesn't Happen"
- "The Only Program Where You Pay $0 If You Don't Win"
- "We Refund You If You Don't [Dream Outcome] In [Time]"

Body must cover:
- Clear criteria (what triggers refund)
- Percentage who get money back (creates realistic expectation)
- What happens if they do hit criteria (testimonial + next step)

#### Giveaway
Headline templates:
- "Win [High-Value Prize] + 5 Runners-Up Get [Lesser Prize]"
- "[Number] Spots Remaining To Enter"
- "Free Entry Closes [Date]"

Body must cover:
- Prize value + why it's life-changing
- Simple entry process
- Winner reveal date
- What runners-up get (drives non-winner conversions)

#### Decoy Offer
Headline templates:
- "Why Smart [Avatar]s Pick [Premium] Over [Basic]"
- "For $X More, You Get 5× The Value"
- "The Secret Most [Avatar]s Miss: [Premium Feature]"

Body must cover:
- Direct comparison table
- Why "small price difference = huge value jump"
- Social proof from premium users

#### Buy X Get Y Free
Headline templates:
- "Buy [X] Get [Y] Free — Limited [Time/Quantity]"
- "Your [Y] Is On Us When You [X]"
- "Bundle Savings: [X] + [Y] = [Lower Price]"

Body must cover:
- Value breakdown ("normally $X + $Y = $Z, today $X")
- Why the bundle makes sense (related benefit)
- Urgency (deadline or quantity)

#### Pay Less Now / More Later
Headline templates:
- "Pay $X Today Or $Y In 90 Days — Your Choice"
- "Early Bird: $X. Regular: $Y"
- "Save $Z When You Decide Today"

Body must cover:
- Why delay costs more (value of decision speed)
- Same delivery either way
- Psychological framing (you pay either way)

### Upsell Copy Patterns

#### Classic Upsell (checkout)
Copy principles:
- Short (1 sentence + CTA)
- Problem awareness: "Most customers also add..."
- Frictionless: one click to add

Templates:
- "Add [X] for $Y and save $Z"
- "Upgrade to premium — most choose this"
- "Add [X]? (Recommended)"

#### Menu Upsell
Copy principles:
- Visual comparison
- Label best option "Most Popular"
- Show savings on larger options

Template:
```
[BASIC] $X - [3 features]
[COMBO] $Y - [7 features] ← MOST POPULAR
[DELUXE] $Z - [12 features] - Save $Q
```

#### Anchor Upsell
Copy principles:
- Lead with anchor price
- Main offer becomes "reasonable"
- Anchor must be genuinely valuable

Template:
```
🥇 VIP PACKAGE — $5000
   Full 1-on-1, custom everything, direct phone line
🥈 PREMIUM — $2000 (← Most choose this)
   Everything essential, group support, weekly calls
🥉 BASIC — $500
   Self-service, community, templates only
```

#### Rollover Upsell
Copy templates:
- "Your $X Rolls Into Our $Y Program"
- "Apply Your Existing Credit To Upgrade"
- "Don't Start Over — Continue With Your $X Credit"

### Downsell Copy Patterns

#### Payment Plan Downsell
Copy principles:
- NEVER frame as "cheaper"
- Frame as "budget-friendly installments"
- Same total value

Template:
- "Prefer installments? 3× $1099 over 3 months"
- "Fits your monthly budget: 3× $X instead of 1× $Y"
- "Same program, easier cash flow"

#### Trial With Penalty
Copy templates:
- "$0 Today. Small $X Fee Only If You Don't [Action]"
- "Try Risk-Free For [Time]. Penalty Only If [Unmet Condition]"
- "[Action] To Unlock Full Access — $X Fee If You Skip"

Body must explain:
- What triggers penalty (clearly)
- Why penalty exists (filters unserious)
- How easy it is to avoid

#### Feature Downsell
Copy templates:
- "Budget Version: $X (without [features])"
- "Core Package: Essentials Only, $X"
- "Starter Tier: [What's Included] — $X"

### Continuity Copy Patterns

#### Continuity Bonus
Copy templates:
- "Join Now, Get $X Bonus Free"
- "Founding Members Get [Bonus] When They Start"
- "First 100 Members Receive [Bonus Package]"

Body must cover:
- Bonus value explicit ($X value)
- Why it's free (celebrating launch, onboarding gift)
- Urgency if limited

#### Continuity Discount
Copy templates:
- "Members Pay Only $X — Half The Public Rate"
- "Lock In $X Rate For Life"
- "Founding Member Pricing: $X/mo"

Body must cover:
- Public price comparison
- Rate lock language ("locked forever")
- Scarcity (founding status)

#### Waived Fee
Copy templates:
- "Setup Fee Waived For 6+ Month Members"
- "$X Onboarding Free When You Commit To [Term]"
- "First Month Free Setup — Stay To Earn It"

### Copy Order Matters
Present offers in THIS order on landing pages:
1. Attraction hook (headline + CTA)
2. Anchor if used
3. Main offer
4. Upsell options as add-ons
5. Payment options (including plan downsell)
6. Guarantees/trial with penalty
7. Continuity option

### Common Copy Mistakes
- ❌ Leading with price before value
- ❌ No anchor → main offer seems expensive
- ❌ Discount language for downsells
- ❌ Burying continuity option
- ❌ Generic "buy now" CTA
- ❌ No comparison for decoy offers
- ❌ Unclear criteria for "money back" offers

### CZ/SK Copy Tweaks
- Use "Peníze zpět" for money-back
- "Platíte pouze pokud" for trial with penalty
- Present plans as "splátky" not "rozložení ceny" (first sounds neutral)
- Avoid aggressive urgency ("Posledních X!") — CZ reads as spam
- Use "Dnes" vs "Zítra" for time-based anchor
- Trust stack (certifications, reviews) near CTA matters more than US

### Master Offer Stack Template (for landing page)
```
[HEADLINE — attraction offer]
[Subhead — what they get]
[Proof — testimonials, numbers]
[Offer breakdown — with anchor]
[Bonus stack — for premium]
[Guarantee — money back or trial]
[Payment options — plan downsell included]
[Continuity option — for after delivery]
[CTA — clear, action-oriented]
[Urgency — deadline or quantity]
[Final risk reversal]
```
