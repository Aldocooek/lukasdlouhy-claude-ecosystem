# Brand Storytelling — Seedance 2.0

Create emotionally resonant brand story videos using Seedance 2.0 on Higgsfield. Brand stories that connect, build trust, and drive engagement through cinematic narrative.

## Platform Specifications

- Best for: Brand narratives, emotional storytelling, cinematic sequences, slow reveals, montage work
- Ideal video length: 60–120 seconds (brand films), 15–30 seconds (social clips)
- Strength: Character moments, authentic dialogue, visual metaphor, emotional depth

## The 2-Second Hook Framework for Brand Stories

The opening frame sets the emotional contract with the viewer. Use one of these 12 patterns:

1. **Provocative Question Hook** — Opens with a direct question that challenges assumptions.
2. **Founder's Hands at Work Hook** — Close-up of hands building, crafting, or creating.
3. **Product-in-World Establishing Shot** — Product appears naturally in someone's life, not staged.
4. **Emotional Human Moment Hook** — Gesture of connection in the first 1–2 seconds.
5. **Surprising Statistic or Contradiction Hook** — Stark visual contrast reveals an unexpected truth.
6. **Time-Lapse Transformation Hook** — Seed to plant, blank canvas to masterpiece.
7. **Unexpected Connection Hook** — Two unrelated moments cut together to reveal shared meaning.
8. **The Problem Hook** — Open on friction, pain, or an unmet need. No brand yet.
9. **Cultural Moment Hook** — Brand at the intersection of a meaningful moment or celebration.
10. **Sensory Hook** — Begin with texture, sound, or tactile experience.
11. **Silhouette or Shadow Hook** — Backlit figure creating mystery before the reveal.
12. **Continuity Hook** — Open with the video already in motion — action started before we arrived.

## Brand Storytelling Philosophy

**Narrative Arc — mandatory structure:**
- Act 1 (Setup): Establish the world and the protagonist's problem.
- Act 2 (Conflict): Tension, struggle, or the moment of change.
- Act 3 (Resolution): The brand's role in reaching a better outcome.

**Emotional Connection Over Information** — Lead with feeling, not features. The emotional hook must land before any product claim.

**Show, Don't Tell** — Let visuals reveal truth. A founder at their workbench at 2am says more than any voiceover about dedication.

**Authenticity as Competitive Advantage** — Real imperfection beats polished artifice. Candid moments outperform scripted ones for trust-building.

**The Hero's Journey: Customer is the hero, brand is the mentor.** The story is about their transformation, not your product.

**Tension and Resolution** — Without tension there is no story. Something must be at stake.

**Values Made Visible** — Embody values in behavior shown on screen, not narrated in voiceover.

## Script Structure Templates

### 60-Second Brand Film
- 0:00–0:02 — Hook (emotional, sensory, or question-based)
- 0:02–0:15 — Setup: the world before the brand. The problem or unmet need.
- 0:15–0:35 — Conflict: the struggle, the turning point, the moment of realization.
- 0:35–0:50 — Resolution: transformation. Brand as the enabler, not the star.
- 0:50–0:58 — Emotional payoff: the outcome in the customer's life.
- 0:58–1:00 — Brand mark + tagline.

### 30-Second Social Brand Story
- 0:00–0:02 — Hook
- 0:02–0:10 — Problem (relatable friction)
- 0:10–0:22 — Transformation moment
- 0:22–0:28 — Emotional resolution
- 0:28–0:30 — Brand mark

## Prompt Engineering for Seedance 2.0

Structure each scene prompt as: `[Camera move] [Subject] [Emotional state] [Setting] [Lighting] [Narrative function]`

Example:
> Slow dolly back from a close-up of a woman's hands wrapping a handmade gift. Her face is not shown. Warm kitchen light, late evening. The shot establishes care and intention before we see anything about the brand.

Keep prompts under 100 words per scene. Name the emotional function of each shot — Seedance responds to emotional intent, not just visual description.

## Common Mistakes

- Leading with the product instead of the problem. Viewers disengage before the emotional connection forms.
- Narrator explaining what visuals should show. Cut the voiceover; show it instead.
- Brand mark appearing too early. Trust must be earned before the brand is revealed.
- Polished production hiding authentic moments. A genuine reaction outperforms a perfect one.
- Treating the brand as the hero. Center the customer's transformation.
